Indian Naan

Description

A delicious flatbread that goes well with soups and curries, Naan is traditionally baked in a clay oven pot, but this recipe uses handy alternatives.

Ingredients

  • 100 grams water
  • 200 grams refined wheat flour
  • a pinch of salt
  • a stick of butter
  • a teaspoon of sugar
  • 2 grams Yeast (optional)

Steps

  1. Whisk the warm water with the yeast and sugar until the yeast is dissolved. Cover and let stand in a warm place for 10 minutes.
  2. Sift flour and salt three times into a large bowl add the yeast mixture, half of the butter and all the yogurt. Mix into a soft dough then knead on a floured surface for about 5 minutes or until dough is smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a large greased bowl, cover and let stand in a warm place for 1 1/2 hours or until the dough is doubled in size.
  3. Punch down dough then knead for 5 minutes. Divide dough into 2 pieces. Roll each piece out into 8 inch round naans.
  4. Cover an oven tray with foil and grease the foil. Brush the naan with a little of the remaining butter and sprinkle with some of the kalonji. Cook naan one at a time under the broiler for about 2 minutes on each side or until puffed and just browned.
